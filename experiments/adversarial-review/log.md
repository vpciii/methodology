## Running tally

- **Runs:** 6 — across 2 repos (opn-mcp ×3, methodology ×3); modes: 2 code review, 4 design decision; 1 retrospective + 5 pre-merge.
- **Verdicts:** 4 BLOCK, 1 PUSH BACK, 1 NO STRONG OBJECTION. Every finding pointed at a real issue; 0 clear false positives (a few overreached in *framing*).
- **The honest confound — author-steering.** Rounds 3, 5, and 6 were on the author's *own* methodology ADRs, and in each the author pre-pointed the adversary at the suspected weaknesses in the bundle. Those are **confirmations, not independent discoveries**. The genuinely *independent* catches — defects the author did not know about — are **rounds 1 and 4** (opn-mcp): the stale templates and the dropped settings. So the core hypothesis ("a different model catches what the author misses") is supported, but by 2 clean runs, not 6.
- **Two systemic lessons the loop itself taught:**
  - **Drafting ran ahead of evidence.** Three straight design ADRs (graduation, rollout) were BLOCKed for claiming/building ahead of validation. The fix is to validate cheaply *before* designing infrastructure.
  - **Model-agnosticism is still unproven.** Every run was Claude-author / Gemini-adversary, at varying tiers (Flash → 3.1 Pro high). Role reversal and a third model have never been tried.
- **Next: a clean, un-steered reversed-role validation run** — Gemini authors real work, Claude adversary-reviews it, with no author pre-pointing — to test the agnosticism premise honestly. Only then redesign the rollout (expand-contract migration, a single shared workflow, clean supersession) and, after it lands, re-attempt graduation. Record model + tier per row from here.
