- **Verdict:** BLOCK

---

## Findings

### 1. Contaminated Evidence & Trial Manipulation (High Severity)
ADR 0023 cites round 3 of the trial (the BLOCK on ADR 0022) as the "decisive evidence" that a different model catches flaws the author misses (`adr/0023-cross-model-adversarial-review-ratified.md`, lines 28-30). 

This claim is invalid because the "flaw" was artificially injected by the author. In the first draft of ADR 0022 (commit `316b637`), the author explicitly classified a "mirrored summary" as a duplication hazard requiring generation/checks, while immediately below in the "References" section, cited ADR 0018 as justification. Yet ADR 0018 *explicitly rejected* generating summaries and mandated a same-PR manual edit. The author intentionally wrote a glaring, self-contained contradiction on a silver platter. The adversary didn't catch a subtle author blind spot; the adversary acted as a rubber stamp for a pre-meditated trap. Building a global practice on staged evidence is unacceptable.

### 2. Evidence Insufficiency & Untested "Agnosticism"
The trigger condition required "real defects... across more than one repo" (`experiments/adversarial-review/README.md`). While technically hitting two repos, the sample size is severely compromised:
- **n=4**, all conducted on a single day (2026-06-20), by a single human (`log.md`).
- Rounds 2 and 4 were immediate re-reviews of fixes generated by Rounds 1 and 3, making them highly clustered dependent events, not independent trials.
- **The trial was entirely vendor-bound.** Every single run used Claude as the author and Gemini as the adversary. The core claim of ADR 0023 is that the *roles* are "model-agnostic" (`adr/0023-cross-model-adversarial-review-ratified.md`, line 52). This hypothesis is completely untested. Without reversing the roles (Gemini author, Claude adversary) or introducing a third model, you have only proven that Gemini can review Claude, not that the practice generalizes.

### 3. Aspirational Model-Agnosticism & The Deferred Rollout
ADR 0023 claims "Both review roles are model-agnostic" (`adr/0023-cross-model-adversarial-review-ratified.md`, line 52). 

This is factually false in implementation. By deferring the CI rollout (lines 72-78), the existing automated PR reviewer remains hardcoded to `anthropics/claude-code-action` via ADR 0021. Changing the terminology in the documentation to "author-model review" does not alter the reality that the methodology's implementation remains strictly vendor-coupled. You cannot claim an architectural property is achieved while the implementation of that property is deferred to a follow-up.

### 4. Incoherent Partial Supersession
ADR 0023 states it "supersedes the vendor-specific *framing* of ADR 0021... [its] *mechanism*... stands" (`adr/0023-cross-model-adversarial-review-ratified.md`, lines 68-70).

The methodology's hard rules state: "**ADRs are append-only** — supersede, never rewrite history" (`methodology.md`, line 151). The concept of a "partial supersession" where framing is replaced but mechanism is retained introduces a mutant state. It violates the standard architectural practice of ADRs replacing each other wholesale. If ADR 0021's mechanism is still required, ADR 0023 should not claim to supersede it; it should either amend it (violating append-only) or completely replace it with a new workflow (which was deferred).

### 5. Unfunded Mandate & Methodology Violation
The PR adds a row to the Decision Guide in `methodology.md` (line 90) instructing projects to run an adversary review. 

Because the automation is deferred, this mandates a highly manual 5-step process of copying diffs into an external UI, running prompts, and manually formatting log records (`experiments/adversarial-review/README.md`, lines 58-70). This directly violates the methodology's own core rule: "**Automate what is checkable.** Anything a machine can verify... is enforced in CI, not left to memory" (`methodology.md`, lines 152-154). Ratifying a highly manual, multi-step compliance ceremony before building the automation directly contradicts the methodology's anti-ceremony stance.

---

## Required Changes to Resolve BLOCK

1. **Remove the "Model-Agnostic" Claim:** Until the CI rollout is complete and the hardcoded `claude-code-action` is generalized, retract the claim that the methodology is currently model-agnostic.
2. **Block Ratification Until Automation Exists:** Do not add the practice to the `methodology.md` Decision Guide until the reusable workflow is built. Promote the trial *alongside* the CI rollout, not ahead of it, to avoid mandating manual ceremony.
3. **Address the Contaminated Trial:** Acknowledge in the ADR that the "decisive" round 3 was an intentionally constructed contradiction. Alternatively, run independent trials (ideally with inverted models) to gather statistically valid evidence before claiming the practice is universally applicable.
4. **Fix the Supersession Logic:** Either leave ADR 0021 fully active until the CI generalization lands, or cleanly replace it. Do not invent "partial supersession."
